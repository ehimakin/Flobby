# Clayboy: Final Silhouette and Proportions

## Approved Concept Reference

The current approved visual reference is [`clayboy-concept-v1.png`](./clayboy-concept-v1.png). It is the working source for proportions, silhouette, material treatment, and facial cavity placement. Refinements may be made during animation and sprite production without replacing the underlying character direction.

## Core Silhouette

Clayboy is a single roughly round lump of clay with no permanent limbs, neck, torso separation, clothing, or accessories. His outline must remain recognisable when rendered as a flat black silhouette.

- Normalised body width: `100%`.
- Normalised total height: `105%`, including the soft head peak.
- Main body mass: approximately `92%` of total height.
- Widest point: around `55%` of body height.
- Base: subtly flattened across the lower `18%`, allowing him to rest against floors and ceilings without appearing spherical.
- Left and right contours: slightly unequal, with soft hand-modelled bulges rather than mirrored curves.
- Head peak: broad, rounded, and compressed; occupies the upper `13-16%` of the silhouette.
- Peak tip: never sharp or conical. Its width should be at least `20%` of the body width.
- Profile depth: approximately `88-92%` of the front-view width.

The peak takes inspiration from the broad rising mass of the supplied Goron mask reference, but is shorter, softer, less triangular, and made from one continuous clay volume.

## Face Placement

The face is formed entirely from hollowed clay indentations. There are no separate eyeballs, teeth, lips, nose, fabric elements, or inserted materials.

### Eyes

- Two simple oval-to-round recessed sockets.
- Eye width: approximately `12-14%` of body width each.
- Eye height: approximately `14-16%` of body height.
- Eye centres: approximately `34%` and `66%` across the front view.
- Vertical centre: approximately `43%` down from the peak.
- The sockets should be widely spaced, dark through natural cavity shadow, and slightly asymmetrical.
- Expressions come from deformation around the sockets rather than pupils or eyebrows.

### Mouth

- One low, wide recessed opening inspired by the supplied doll expression proportions.
- Mouth width: approximately `38-42%` of body width.
- Mouth height when neutral: approximately `10%` of body height.
- Mouth height when shocked or exerting: up to `18%`.
- Mouth centre: approximately `68%` down the body.
- Shape: soft horizontal oval or rounded trapezoid, with an uneven hand-pressed rim.
- The mouth cavity is clay shadow, not a painted black shape.

## Material Language

- Matte, tactile modelling clay.
- Subtle fingerprints, compression marks, and kneading seams.
- Rounded edges with no hard manufactured symmetry in Normal form.
- Provisional concept colour: warm natural terracotta clay.
- State changes must alter silhouette and material, not rely on colour alone.

## Animation Constraints

- Clayboy may squash to `75%` of normal height and stretch to `125%` during movement.
- The peak compresses and reforms with the body rather than behaving like hair.
- Facial cavities deform with the clay volume while remaining readable.
- Rolling may temporarily displace the face, but it should settle back to the front.
- Edge stopping should visibly compress the supported side and allow the leading `45%` to overhang.

## Avoid

- A sharp cone-shaped head.
- A perfect circle or symmetrical teardrop.
- Permanent arms, legs, ears, or a separate head.
- Button eyes, pupils, teeth, a zipper, stitching, or fabric texture.
- Directly reproducing either supplied reference character.
