# Clayboy: Opening Cinematic

Night presses against the windows of a crooked old house. Deep inside, an evil sculptor works beneath the orange glare of a kiln. Around him stand rows of grotesque clay creatures: twisted beasts, crawling hands, and half-finished guardians. Above the workbench, a tiny fairy hangs imprisoned inside a glass jar. Threads of her light are pulled through copper tubes and forced into each sculpture, bringing it shuddering to life.

The sculptor slams another lump of clay onto the table. The fairy studies it, then closes her eyes. Her light enters the clay, but no monster emerges. It remains a small, round, unremarkable shape.

Disgusted, the sculptor throws the failed lump onto a scrap pile.

Two eyes open.

The little clay figure rolls free and sees the workshop for the first time. He sees the creatures. He sees the jar. The fairy meets his gaze and gives the faintest pulse of light. Though he does not know her name, he understands that she made him this way deliberately.

He rolls towards her, gathering speed to strike the jar. A huge hand crashes down. Clayboy dodges, bounces across the workbench, and escapes through an open window.

Outside, he lands in wet grass beneath the towering house. The window glows far above him. Clayboy looks back, reshapes himself, and begins the long journey inside.
