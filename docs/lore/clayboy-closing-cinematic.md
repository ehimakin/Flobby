# Clayboy: Closing Cinematic

The workshop lies in ruins. His creations broken and his power failing, the Sculptor snatches the Fairy's jar from its stand and stuffs it into his breast pocket. If he cannot win, he will take the source of his magic and flee.

Clayboy blocks his path.

With a roar, the Sculptor swings his open hand to flatten him once again. But Clayboy remembers. At the final instant, his soft body pulls upward into a hardened cone. The Sculptor's palm crashes down upon its point.

He recoils, clutching his bleeding hand. "My hand! My instrument of creation... ruined!"

He staggers towards the bathroom, desperate to nurse it beneath the tap. Clayboy follows. When the Sculptor sees him, humiliation becomes rage. He charges without thinking.

Clayboy sees the open toilet behind him. He sees the Sculptor's approaching shoe. Then he throws himself beneath it.

The step lands. Clayboy spreads across the tile like clay beneath a rolling pin, interrupting the Sculptor's stride. The Sculptor stumbles towards the bowl. Too precious about his injured hands to catch himself, he refuses to put them out.

He falls face-first into the blocked toilet.

Silence.

Clayboy lies across the floor as a thin, motionless smear. No eyes open. No shape returns.

The Fairy's jar slips from the unconscious Sculptor's pocket. Its enchanted glass strikes the tile and cracks. It does not shatter, but for the first time there is a path through its magic seal.

For years, the Sculptor had forced the Fairy to sneeze, harvesting the dust that shook from her wings and using it to animate his grotesque clay creations. But the magic she gave Clayboy was never ordinary fairy dust. She had placed something deliberate within that unfinished lump: a concentrated piece of her own essence, shaped not to impose a form, but to give him the freedom to choose every form for himself.

Now that same light streams through the crack.

It moves across the tiles, finding every scattered trace of Clayboy. The smear trembles. Clay gathers towards clay, folding and rising beneath the Fairy's glow until a small, round figure sits before the broken jar.

Two eyes open.

Clayboy presses himself into the crack, widens inside it, and carefully parts the enchanted glass. The Fairy rises free at last. She looks at the little figure the Sculptor once dismissed as waste, and Clayboy looks back at the friend whose name he still does not know.

Behind them, the Sculptor remains unconscious in the toilet he once meant to use as Clayboy's grave.
